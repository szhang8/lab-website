<p>I love photography! I am pretty&nbsp;into the genre of photojournalism/street photography. To me, the most soul-touching photos are always on the&nbsp;topic the&nbsp;capturing human&nbsp;conditions. There is tremendous humanity embedded in those photos that go beyond beautiful colors and sceneries. I brought a lot of&nbsp;photography books. One of the recent ones is called "The Family Imprint". What's special about this book is Nancy, the author and the photographer, used her&nbsp;lens to capture the last couple of years when both her parents were diagnosed with stage-four cancers and treated at the same time. I finally sat down last night and read through this book page&nbsp;by page.&nbsp;</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><br>It was feeling quite heavy when reading this book. You can find some of the photographs in the book here: <a rel="noreferrer noopener" href="http://www.nancyborowick.com/the-family-imprint/the-photographs/tfi_01/" target="_blank">http://www.nancyborowick.com/the-family-imprint/the-photographs/tfi_01/</a>&nbsp;At the end of this book, there are some bright notes. I like the positive&nbsp;attitudes and sense of fearlessness, even facing the most devastating&nbsp;events in one's life.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
